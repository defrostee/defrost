GitHub Pages
Free // No install required // Instant
Progress Step 1 of 6
Step 01 of 06
Fork the repository
Go to the Defrost GitHub repo. In the top-right corner of the page, click the Fork button. This copies the project into your own GitHub account. If you don't have an account, sign up here first — it's free!
Step 02 of 06
Star the repository
While you're there, click the Star ⭐ button at the top of the page too. It helps the project grow and means a lot! You can find the star button right next to the Fork button.
Tip: Starring is optional but super appreciated!
Step 03 of 06
Open your fork's Settings
Go to your forked repo (it'll be at github.com/YOUR-USERNAME/defrost). Click the Settings tab near the top of the page.
Step 04 of 06
Go to Pages
In the left sidebar inside Settings, scroll down and click Pages. This is where you turn on your free website.
Step 05 of 06
Enable GitHub Pages
Under Branch, click the dropdown that says None and select main. Leave the folder as / (root). Click Save.
Tip: It takes about 1–2 minutes to go live after you save!
Step 06 of 06
Visit your site!
Refresh the Pages settings after a minute or two. GitHub will show you a link at the top that says "Your site is live at..." — click it and you're done! Your URL will look like YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/defrost.
You're live! 🎉
Your site is now on the internet via GitHub Pages.

Netlify
Free // Custom name // Live in seconds
Progress Step 1 of 6
Step 01 of 06
Fork the repository
Go to the Defrost GitHub repo and click Fork in the top-right. This copies it to your GitHub account. No account? Sign up free here.
Step 02 of 06
Sign up for Netlify
Go to netlify.com and click Sign up. Choose Sign up with GitHub — this makes connecting your repo way easier. Authorize Netlify when it asks.
Step 03 of 06
Add a new site
Once logged in, click Add new site → Import an existing project. Choose GitHub as your provider, then find and click your forked defrost repo from the list.
Step 04 of 06
Deploy the site
Netlify will show you some settings — you don't need to change anything here. Just scroll down and click Deploy site. It'll start building. Wait about 5–10 seconds.
Step 05 of 06
Rename your site
After deploying, go to Site configuration in the left menu. Scroll down just a tiny bit until you see Site details. Click Change site name, type whatever name you want, and save. Your URL will become your-name.netlify.app.
Tip: Names must be unique — if yours is taken, try adding numbers or dashes.
Step 06 of 06
Visit your site!
Wait about 5 seconds, then click your Netlify URL at the top of the dashboard — it'll look like your-name.netlify.app. Your site is live!
You're live! 🎉
Your site is now on the internet via Netlify.

InfinityFree
Free // Custom domain // ZIP upload
Progress Step 1 of 9
Step 01 of 09
Download the files as a ZIP
Go to the Defrost GitHub repo. Click the green Code button, then click Download ZIP. Save it somewhere easy to find like your Desktop.
Step 02 of 09
Create an InfinityFree account
Go to infinityfree.com and click Register. Enter your email and a password, then verify your email when they send you a confirmation.
Step 03 of 09
Create a hosting account & domain
After logging in, click New Account. You'll be asked to choose a subdomain — type whatever you want (like mysite) and InfinityFree will give you a free URL like mysite.infinityfreeapp.com. Click Create Account and wait a minute.
Step 04 of 09
Open the File Manager
From your account dashboard, click Control Panel on your hosting account. Inside the control panel, find and click File Manager. If there's no folder listed yet, click Create Directory first, then open File Manager.
Step 05 of 09
Navigate to htdocs
Inside the File Manager, open the folder called htdocs. This is the folder that holds your website files. Everything you upload here will show up on your site.
Step 06 of 09
Upload your ZIP
Click Upload at the top of the file manager. Then click the Zip & Extract tab. Click Upload again and select the ZIP file you downloaded in Step 1. Wait for it to finish uploading.
Step 07 of 09
Extract & Refresh
After uploading, find your ZIP file in the list. Under the Actions column next to it, click Extract. Once it finishes, click Refresh (also under Actions or the refresh button at the top) to see your extracted files appear.
Step 08 of 09
Check for the green checkmark
Go back to your InfinityFree dashboard tab and refresh the page. Look at your hosting account — check that Index File shows a green checkmark ✓. If it's not green yet, wait 2 minutes and refresh again.
No green check? Make sure your main file inside htdocs is named exactly index.html — lowercase, no spaces.
Step 09 of 09
Open your link!
Once you see the green checkmark, click your domain URL from the dashboard. Your site is live! If it doesn't load, wait 2 more minutes and try again — it just needs a moment to fully activate.
To update: Go back to File Manager, delete the old files, and upload a new ZIP.
You're live! 🎉
Your site is now on the internet via InfinityFree.
